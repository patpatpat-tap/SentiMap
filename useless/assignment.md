## Part 1 – In Your Own Words

1) What is system availability?
System availability is the proportion of time a system is operational and accessible to users. It measures how reliably a service can be used when needed.

2) Why is system availability important for online enrollment systems?
Online enrollment is time-sensitive and often has strict deadlines, so outages can prevent students from enrolling. High availability reduces missed enrollments and support incidents.

3) What is the difference between downtime and availability?
Downtime is the period when a system is not functioning or reachable. Availability is the overall measure of how much time the system is up versus down.

## Part 3 – Availability Computation

Formula:
Availability = Uptime / (Uptime + Downtime)

Given:
Uptime = 168 - 3 = 165 hours
Downtime = 3 hours

Compute:
Availability = 165 / (165 + 3) = 165 / 168 = 0.982142857
Availability percentage = 98.21%

Is this high availability? Why?
It is relatively high because the system is available more than 98% of the time, but it is not "high availability" by strict standards (often 99.9%+). For critical systems, 3 hours of weekly downtime is still significant.
