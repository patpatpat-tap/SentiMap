# Async Activity

Title of Research Idea: SentiMap: Hybrid NLP and Geospatial Analysis for Mapping Cebu City Traffic Enforcement Grievances
Target Users / Setting: Cebu City traffic authorities, urban planners, and researchers using a dashboard in a civic operations setting

Paragraph 1 (What YOU say - Context / Background)
Cebu City experiences recurring traffic enforcement issues that citizens report informally on social media, especially Reddit. These posts describe congestion, unfair enforcement, safety risks, and sarcastic reactions that rarely appear in formal complaint channels. This creates a gap between what the public experiences and what decision makers can see. SentiMap responds to this by converting citizen narratives into structured signals that can be mapped and summarized. The setting is a local civic context where quick, evidence based insights can guide attention to hotspots and trends. The importance is practical: a reliable, interpretable system can reduce response time, prioritize enforcement resources, and support policy review without waiting for slow or fragmented reporting.

Paragraph 2 (What OTHERS say - Related studies / What is known)
Prior work shows that social media text can be classified reliably with accuracy based evaluation when clear labels and baselines are used. Studies on Indonesian Twitter and product reviews report strong performance when comparing classical baselines to Transformer models (Mayzaroh et al., 2026; Zahra et al., 2026). Other research demonstrates that aspect based sentiment on Reddit can be evaluated using shared task benchmarks with F1 metrics (Zhu et al., 2026). Review based studies in app and music platforms also emphasize accuracy and weighted F1 for imbalanced classes (Maharani et al., 2026; Purba et al., 2026). Together, these findings suggest that well designed benchmarking, consistent preprocessing, and clear reporting of accuracy and F1 can validate sentiment systems on noisy user generated text.

Paragraph 3 (Gap - What is missing)
Despite these advances, there is limited work that focuses on Cebuano or Bislish traffic discourse with sarcasm, slang, and location references. Generic sentiment tools trained on English or formal datasets often fail to capture local meaning, and most geospatial analyses are not tuned to the linguistic realities of Cebu City. There is also a practical gap: existing systems rarely connect sentiment accuracy to actionable hotspot mapping in a single pipeline. For Cebu traffic enforcement, it is not enough to label sentiment; the system must also extract locations, filter noise, and present results in a form that authorities can interpret quickly.

Paragraph 4 (What to do with the gap - Purpose / Direction)
This study will build and evaluate a hybrid NLP pipeline tailored to Cebuano and Bislish traffic grievances, then connect it to geospatial clustering for hotspot visualization. It will use a curated Reddit dataset, apply sentiment, sarcasm, emotion, and location extraction, and validate performance with accuracy and F1 metrics on a manually labeled subset. The output will be a map centered dashboard that highlights complaint intensity and supports review of individual grievances. By combining local language adaptation with geospatial analytics, the study addresses both the linguistic and operational gaps and provides a credible, defensible tool for local decision making.
